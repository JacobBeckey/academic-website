    ★ John Preskill — Quantum Computation and Information Lecture Notes
    Online lecture notes
    Intermediate
    Physics

    A classic set of lecture notes that develops quantum information and basic quantum computing from a physics point of view. Good for students who are comfortable with linear algebra and Dirac notation and want a more conceptual, calculation-oriented introduction.

    John Preskill — Quantum Computation and Information Lecture Notes
    Online lecture notes
    Intermediate
    Physics

    A classic set of lecture notes that develops quantum information and basic quantum computing from a physics point of view. Good for students who are comfortable with linear algebra and Dirac notation and want a more conceptual, calculation-oriented introduction.

    Nielsen & Chuang — Quantum Computation and Quantum Information

    Book (Cambridge University Press) Intermediate Mixed

    The standard reference in quantum information. Best approached after you have some comfort with linear algebra and basic quantum mechanics. Use it as a “backbone” text and supplement with lecture notes or more focused resources as needed.

    Mark Wilde — Quantum Information Theory

    Book + freely available draft Advanced Math

    A mathematically focused treatment of quantum Shannon theory: channels, capacities, and entropic quantities. Well-suited for students with a strong analysis / probability background who want to see the information-theoretic core of the subject.

    Linear Algebra & Background Mathematics

    Sheldon Axler — Linear Algebra Done Right

    Book (Springer) Intro Math

    A proof-based introduction to linear algebra that emphasizes linear maps and eigenstructure over matrix manipulation. Great for students transitioning into more theoretical coursework (and very aligned with how I teach 416).

    Roman Vershynin — High-Dimensional Probability: An Introduction with Applications in Data Science

    Book + free draft Advanced Math

    An introduction to concentration inequalities, random matrices, and probabilistic tools that appear frequently in modern quantum information, learning theory, and high-dimensional analysis.

    Probability, Statistics & Learning Theory

    Shai Shalev-Shwartz & Shai Ben-David — Understanding Machine Learning: From Theory to Algorithms

    Book + free PDF Intermediate CS

    A clean introduction to statistical learning theory, sample complexity, and core algorithmic ideas. Helpful background if you’re interested in quantum learning theory and want to understand the classical side carefully first.

    David Williams — Probability with Martingales

    Book (Cambridge University Press) Advanced Math

    A rigorous probability text that builds measure-theoretic ideas through martingales. Not required for most undergrad projects, but very useful if you’re heading toward research that mixes quantum information with serious probability.

    Computer Science Foundations

    Michael Sipser — Introduction to the Theory of Computation

    Standard textbook Intermediate CS

    A standard reference for automata, computability, and complexity. Helpful if you want to understand how quantum complexity classes (like BQP and QMA) fit into the broader landscape.

    Oded Goldreich — Introduction to Property Testing (survey and lecture notes)

    Survey / notes Advanced CS

    A gateway into property testing, which is closely related to many quantum state-testing and learning-theoretic questions. Best once you are comfortable with discrete probability and basic complexity theory.
